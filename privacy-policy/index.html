Privacy Policy

Craft & Being A brand of Mendoza Collective Co. Last updated: July 6, 2026

Craft & Being ("we," "us," "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information of visitors to craftandbeing.com (the "Site") and our clients. This policy explains what we collect, why, and how you can reach us with questions.

We are based in British Columbia, Canada, and handle personal information in accordance with the federal Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and, where applicable, BC's Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA).

This policy should be read alongside our Terms of Use, which govern your use of this Site, our digital products, and our content — including restrictions on AI training and scraping.

1. Information We Collect

Information you provide directly:

  • Name, email address, and any details you share when you submit our contact form
  • Information you share with us in the course of a client project (e.g. brand details, project materials, communications)

Information collected automatically:

  • Usage data collected via Google Analytics, such as pages visited, time on site, device/browser type, and general location (city/region level, derived from IP address)

We do not collect payment information through this Site.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Respond to inquiries submitted through our contact form
  • Deliver, manage, and communicate about client projects
  • Understand how visitors use the Site, so we can improve it
  • Meet legal and accounting obligations

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use it for purposes beyond those described here without your consent.

3. How Your Information Is Shared

Contact form submissions are processed through Formspree, a third-party form service, and delivered to our Gmail inbox. Formspree processes this data on our behalf as a service provider and is subject to its own privacy practices.

Analytics data is processed by Google Analytics, which may store data on servers outside Canada, including in the United States.

We may also share information with trusted service providers who support our business operations (e.g. bookkeeping, project management, design or editorial support), solely for the purpose of delivering our services and under obligations to protect your information.

We do not share your personal information with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

4. International Clients

While Craft & Being is based in Canada, we occasionally work with clients located outside Canada and the United States, including in the European Union and United Kingdom. If you are located in a jurisdiction with its own data protection laws (such as the GDPR), you may have additional rights regarding your personal information, including the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your data, and to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. We will honour these rights to the extent they apply, regardless of where you are located.

5. Cookies

Google Analytics uses cookies to collect usage data as described above. You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings, or opt out of Google Analytics tracking using Google's opt-out browser add-on.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, meet our legal and accounting obligations, or maintain an ongoing client relationship. Contact form inquiries that do not lead to a working relationship are retained for a reasonable period and then deleted.

7. Consent

By submitting our contact form or otherwise providing us with personal information, you consent to its collection and use as described in this policy. In some cases, consent may be implied by your conduct (for example, continuing to use the Site after being made aware of this policy). Where we intend to use your information for a new purpose not described here, we will seek your consent before doing so.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Ask what personal information we hold about you
  • Request that we correct or update inaccurate information
  • Request that we delete your personal information, subject to legal or contractual retention requirements
  • Withdraw consent to our use of your information, where applicable

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details below. We will respond within 30 days, as required by law.

9. Challenging Our Compliance

If you believe we have not handled your personal information in accordance with this policy or applicable law, you may raise your concern directly with us at the contact below, and we will investigate. If you remain unsatisfied, you have the right to file a complaint with:

  • The Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner for British Columbia (oipc.bc.ca), and/or
  • The Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (priv.gc.ca)

10. Security and Breach Notification

We take reasonable steps to protect the personal information in our care, including relying on reputable third-party providers (Formspree, Google, our hosting provider) who maintain their own security standards. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a breach that creates a real risk of significant harm to you, we will notify you and the applicable privacy regulator as required by law.

11. Children's Privacy

This Site is not directed at children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect the most recent revision.
